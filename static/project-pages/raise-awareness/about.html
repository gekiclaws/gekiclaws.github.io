How is renewable energy harnessed?

The implementation of renewable energy sources mainly involves the installation or construction of energy harvesting devices for each type of energy source. For instance, solar panels can be installed to convert sunlight into electricity, while concentrating solar power plants can derive heat energy from the sun. Hydropower, the world’s largest renewable energy source, is harnessed through the construction of dams and underwater turbines, which convert the kinetic energy in moving water – rivers and ocean tides – into electricity. Furthermore, wind energy is collected through wind turbines, which convert wind into electricity.

How sustainable is renewable energy?

Sustainable energy and renewable energy are two terms often used interchangeably under the belief that they represent the same idea. However, this is not true, as renewable energy only includes sources that have minimal impact on the environment i.e. nuclear energy is sustainable but not a renewable energy source. Moreover, renewable energy itself not necessarily sustainable, as a truly sustainable energy source should be both environmentally and economically sustainable, and the current costs of harnessing renewable energy remain unsustainable from an economic standpoint.
