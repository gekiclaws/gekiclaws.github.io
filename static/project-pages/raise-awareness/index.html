The environmental impact of fossil fuel use
An overview of the current fossil fuel situation

Since the Industrial Revolution, the global consumption of natural resources has increased, and humans have consumed more natural resources annually than the Earth can regenerate in a year since 1970. Currently, 80% of the world’s energy supply comes from non-renewable fossil fuels, including coal, crude oil, and natural gas, which are sent to power plants to be burned and converted into electricity. However, increasing energy demands have rendered it difficult to limit our usage of fossil fuels and is causing fossil fuel depletion; alternative solutions to supporting global energy demands must be explored. The overuse of fossil fuels has had a significant impact on the environment, resulting in the ever-increasing depletion of fossil fuels, as well as the increasing effects of climate change.
